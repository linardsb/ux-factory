Fictional scenario Verdant is a fictional product, invented for this demonstration. No real company, users, or data are involved. The screenshots are of Verdant's own prototype — so the round-trip has a known answer to measure against.

Read this as a controlled, favourable case. The “ground truth” is the engine's own derive(Verdant axes) output, and reading a solid accent off a clean render is close to a colour-picker operation — so a near-zero accent ΔE is expected and proves the pipeline works end to end, not that derivation is high-fidelity on unfamiliar input.

Neutral tokens are tinted by the engine at sub-perceptual chroma; a vision agent reads them as plain white/grey, so their ΔE looks large but is perceptually trivial — reported for transparency, excluded from the verdict. The real fidelity claim rests on the offline real-product test recorded in the architecture's spikes, not on this number.
