The components

The library, live. Touch anything.

Both prototypes above are built from these. Every card holds the shipped component itself, running here on this page. They read the same token contract the page does, so switching the pack re-skins them along with everything else.

  • vd-plant-card

    A custom element with its own shadow DOM. Press one: it tints, squishes, and fires a vd-select event. The card is a link, which Safari puts in the tab order only when full keyboard access is on. Verdant is a made-up product, invented for this demonstration.

  • vd-status-chip

    All three states at once. The chip reports status and takes no input, so its spec gives it no role and no tab stop. The word carries the state; the colour only adds weight.

  • vd-care-task-row

    A checkbox that reads as a row. Click it or press Space to tick one off. The status chip stays put on purpose: the task is late until the log commits, and ticking it does not change that.

  • ds-metric-tile

    Plants healthy 18 of 21

    Due today 3 tasks

    Overdue 4 tasks

    The one primitive both scenarios share, shown at its three emphasis levels. It is read-only by spec, so it hovers with its card and takes no press. The label and the number say the state on their own; the tone only turns up the volume.

  • .btn-primary / .btn-secondary / .btn-ghost

    The site's own buttons, the same ones under every call to action on this page. The press squish lives inside the component: it scales down on the fast curve and rebounds through the bounce spring. The arrow slides on hover.

  • .vd-primary-button

    The prototype's commit button, enabled and disabled. The disabled one keeps its text and drops to a muted surface, so the reason stays readable rather than greyed into noise.
