The derivation round-trip, in full.

The factory can also run in reverse: give it screenshots and it proposes the design tokens behind them. Here that ran on Verdant's own prototype, where the right answer is known, so the proposal can be measured instead of trusted. This page shows the whole exhibit: what the AI read, what it proposed, how far off it was, and the recorded run itself.

01What was read, and how to read the result

Fictional scenario Verdant is a fictional product, invented for this demonstration. No real company, users, or data are involved. The screenshots are of Verdant's own prototype, so the round-trip has a known answer to measure against.

Read this as a controlled, favourable case. The "ground truth" is the engine's own output for Verdant, and reading a solid accent colour off a clean render is close to a colour-picker operation. A near-zero accent difference proves the pipeline works end to end, not that derivation is high-fidelity on unfamiliar input.

Neutral tokens carry a tint too subtle for the eye, so the AI reads them as plain white or grey. Their measured difference looks large but is invisible in practice: reported for transparency, excluded from the verdict.
